Teaching Experience

Since I am interested in pursuing a career in academia, I applied to be a teaching assistant during my second-year undergrad at the University of Toronto. In Fall 2020, I was a TA for Linear Algebra II, MAT224, taught by Professor Sean Uppal. I led weekly tutorials with a classroom size of 30, held office hours and graded assignments. Every TA in the course is given a handout of practice problems of more than ten questions and is free to choose the format for the tutorial session. Before each tutorial, I would solve all the questions in the handout and choose a few questions to go through during the tutorial. As a second-year undergrad, this was my first time leading a tutorial for a university-level course. Plus, this course focuses on rigorous mathematical proofs. I quickly became confident in teaching and became better at presenting complex proofs.

In Winter 2021, I was a grading TA in Mathematical Expression and Reasoning for Computer Science, CSC165, taught by Professor Francois Pitt, Professor Lindsey Shorser and Professor Kirill Serkh. This course is the foundational Computer Science theory course. In a teaching team of more than 12 TAs, I participated actively in the marking scheme calibration and communicated frequently with my colleagues to ensure consistent marking.

At Marianopolis College in Montreal, I was selected by my Calculus teacher as a grader in 2019. I was also a peer tutor in chemistry in 2018. I love the academic community and enjoy interacting with students and professors to discuss challenging problems.
