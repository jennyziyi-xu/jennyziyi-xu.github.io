Leadership

I am a loyal member of the University of Toronto Computer Science Student Union (CSSU). I joined the CSSU Council as one of the three First-Year Representatives in 2019. In 2020, I was glad to be elected Director of Academic Events. I had a pleasure to host academic seminars with Professor David Duvenaud, Professor Roger Grosse, Professor Sven Dickinson and Professor Eugene Fiume. Tea Chat with Profs is a traditional event of CSSU where we invite professors and all CS student to gather and connect in a relaxing setting. I continued this tradition, and we had an awesome time with Professor Jennifer Campbell, Professor Karen Reid, Professor Joseph Williams, Professor Faith Ellen, Professor Mario Badr, Professor Jaqueline Smith, Professor David Liu, Professor Diane Horton, Professor Jonathan Calver and other professors.

In 2021, I was honoured to be elected President of CSSU. I aimed to make every CS student feel a sense of belonging at U of T. With the executives on the council, we provided 2000 undergrad CS students the opportunity to connect with faculty members and industry leaders through various events. I initiated the connection with the Computer Science Club at Waterloo University to facilitate exchanges between our two universities. Interested in building a global platform for undergraduate CS students, I also sent collaboration invitation to Harvard Computer Society, Stanford ACM, McGill Computer Science Undergrad Society, MIT IEEE/ACM and Princeton Computer Science Council. Please feel free to email me for potential collaboration. Although I am not the President of the CSSU this year, I am more than happy to participate in expanding CSSU and creating a strong bound with Computer Science societies at other universities.

Since May 2021, I have been a Team Lead in 3D Object Detection at aUToronto, The University Of Toronto Self-Driving Car Team. I am proud that our team is the 1st Place Winner of 2022 SAE Autodrive Challenge II and Overall winner of the SAE Autodrive Challenge I Series (2018~2021). We are featured in the UofT Engineering News.

I am glad to connect with talented undergraduate and graduate students on the team. I enjoy lively debates and discussions with my teammates and our faculty supervisors. The autonomous driving stack is a complex system. Every team lead specializes in a sub-system, and every discussion allowed me to gain new perspectives.

I joined the University of Toronto Badminton Club in 2019 as an Exec of the Intramural Team. I still miss intramural leagues every Friday night during my first year in 2019. In 2021, I became the Webmaster of the team. I built the current website from scratch with the support of the team. As a badminton athlete and swimmer since the age of nine, I wish to bring people together through sports.
