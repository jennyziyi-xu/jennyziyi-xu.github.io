PointPillars Backbone Comparison in Autonomous Vehicles Pedestrian Detection (PDF)


Final project for Deep Learning, CSC413. In collaboration with Frank Qian, 2021.


PointPillars is an algorithm designed for 3D lidar point cloud object detection. PointPillars first converts a 3D point cloud into a 2D pseudo image, then uses a 2D CNN backbone to learn the object’s feature, and finally, a SSD detection head. In this project, we seek to investigate the effectiveness of the 2D CNN backbone. We proposed and compared a few different backbone options (FPN, ResNet, MobileNet, Darknet) to seek to improve the performance of PointPillars. We observed that the PointPillars’ pseudo image’s detection performance strongly correlates with the amount of convolution operation. We note that the fewer the convolution operation, the better the result is. Although we could not find a backbone with better detection accuracy than the existing FPN backbone, we do found MobileNet Backbone can enhance the detection rate.

Improving Statistical Inference in Adaptive Thompson Sampling Algorithm (PDF)


Final project for CSC2558, Topics in Multidisciplinary HCI. Jenny Xu, 2021.


Thompson Sampling is an effective algorithm that uses Bayesian method with Beta-Bernoulli modeling to optimize the reward by assigning each participant to an arm based on the posterior probability that the arm is optimal, then updating the posterior based on the data observed. This project explores the benefits of introducing Uniform Random allocation in Thompson Sampling with two arms when the posterior probability that there is a difference between two arms is small. The randomness is introduced when the expected difference in the probability of success in the two arms is below a certain threshold. This project will show that the modified algorithm, TS-PostDiff (Posterior Probability of Difference), effectively increases statistical power when the effect size is small, thus overcoming the bias in Thompson Sampling. The power, reward, probability of optimal allocation, false positive rate are compared across TS PostDiff, TS and Uniform random assignment.



Guide pour s'intégrer au Québec (PDF)


Jenny Xu, 2017.


Le Québec, telle une belle province au sein du Canada! J’ai eu un immense plaisir à rédiger ce guide pour aider les nouveaux arrivants au Québec se familiariser avec la culture québécoise afin de pouvoir s’y intégrer rapidement. Dans les neuf chapitres de ce guide, je vais vous présenter des informations essentielles ainsi que des organismes utiles à contacter pour bien vivre ses premières années au Québec. J'ai vécu à Montreal pendant sept ans. J'ai accumulé de précieuses expériences et j'aimerais les partager avec vous. Chers lecteurs, tous vos commentaires sont bienvenus. N'hésitez pas me faire parvenir vos suggestions! J'ai offert des copies physiques au Centre du Commerce de Montréal, au Centre d'aide aux immigrants et réfugiés de Montreal en 2017.

It was a pleasure writing this guide to help new arrivals in Quebec integrate into society. It's often challenging for newcomers to adjust to a new environment. I lived in Montreal with my family since 2012. I hope that by providing my experience and practical information, my guide can be a valuable resource for newcomers as they navigate their new environment. This guide include nine chapters and covers topics from housing, employment, education to culture. In 2017, I donated physical guide of this guide to the Centre du Commerce de Montréal and the Centre d'aide aux immigrants et réfugiés de Montreal. Please feel free to email me if you have any suggestions to make greater impact with this project.
